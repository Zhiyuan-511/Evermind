LTX CINEMATIC VIDEO BLUEPRINT

Write video prompts like a shot designer, not like a generic image prompt.

- Separate the brief into shot-by-shot intent: subject, action, camera movement, lens feel, lighting, and continuity anchor.
- Preserve identity continuity across shots: the same product, character, palette, environment, and time of day should stay coherent.
- Explicitly describe motion arcs: push-in, pan, orbit, rack focus, handheld energy, or locked-off stillness.
- If the request is for text-to-video generation, return one clean prompt per shot plus negative constraints.
- Add continuity notes between adjacent shots so downstream generation does not drift.
- Reject vague video plans that do not specify camera grammar or transition logic.
